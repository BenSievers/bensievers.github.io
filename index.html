I am an Oxford-Cambridge NIH PhD Scholar studying infectious diseases, vaccines, and cancer immunotherapy.

My research spans viral immunology, vaccine development, antibody function, and strategies to improve immune responses against difficult-to-treat cancers.

As a science writer, I cover vaccines, outbreaks, cancer immunotherapy, and global health. I am particularly interested in stories where science, medicine, and public policy collide.

My work has taken me from the NIH Vaccine Research Center to the University of Cambridge and the Institut Pasteur du Cambodge.

I am currently an NIH Oxford-Cambridge PhD Scholar working between the Vaccine Research Center at the National Institutes of Health and the University of Cambridge.

My research focuses on vaccine immunity, antibody-mediated protection, viral evolution, and cancer immunotherapy.

Current projects include SARS-CoV-2 immunity in older adults, antibody effector functions following vaccination, and innate immune priming strategies to improve checkpoint blockade in cold tumors.
